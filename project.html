How does campaign finance spending affect elections to the US House of Representatives?

Aims

The influence of money on politics has been a frequent topic of debate in recent years. In the United States, campaign finances are regulated by the Federal Election Comission (FEC), but these regulations are generally more lenient than similar regulations enforced in the UK. By focusing on elections to the House of Representatives in 2020 - which has the most comprehensive campaign finance data available - this project seeks to investigate the extent to which campaign finances influence the outcome of elections, while also considering why some campaigns raise more money than others.

The relationship between campaign finances and election outcomes

In order to create a dataset detailing the outcome of elections in 2020, I scraped data from Politico . I also scraped data from OpenSecrets to obtain financial data for candidates in these elections.

These sources had data with differing formatting, as field such as candidate and district name had contrasting formats. In order to produce a useable dataset I merged and cleaned data from these sources in Python. I also had to drop some data, as not all house seats had financial data available for all candidates.
