How does campaign finance spending affect elections to the US House of Representatives?

Aims

The influence of money on politics has been a frequent topic of debate in recent years. In the United States, campaign finances are regulated by the Federal Election Comission (FEC), but these regulations are generally more lenient than similar reegulations enforced in the UK. By focusing on elections to the House of Representatives in 2020 - which has the most comprehensive campaign finance data available - this project seeks to investigate the extent to which campaign finances influence the outcome of elections, while also considering why some campaigns raise more money than others.

I scraped data from Politico to get data on 2020 election outcomes. I then scraped data from OpenSecrets to get financial data for house seats in the 2020 election.
